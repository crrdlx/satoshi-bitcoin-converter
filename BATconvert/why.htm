Why use Ꝼ as the symbol for a Finney?

  • This particular unit of ether needs a symbol. The Finney is at the "Goldilocks" size, at least for now. It's not too big, not too small, but just right for real-life use, like buying a Guinness.
  • Hal Finney was Irish, assumingly by his name. The symbol is the Irish Gaelic F, known as the Insular F.
  • It's already Unicode: details here.
  • It's easy enough to be handwritten.
  • It's a tip 'o the cap homage to the recipient of the first bitcoin transaction.
